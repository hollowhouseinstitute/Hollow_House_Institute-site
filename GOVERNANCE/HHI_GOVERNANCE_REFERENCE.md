## 1. Purpose

This document consolidates **HHI governance principles, frameworks, and standard alignments**. It serves as a **reference guide** for:

- Internal governance implementation.
- External alignment with AI governance standards (NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001).
- Peer review, audit, and standards submissions.
- Training and stakeholder awareness programs.

---

## 2. Governance Principles

HHI governance is built on the following **core principles**:

1. **Transparency:** All AI-human interactions and decision processes must be traceable.  
2. **Accountability:** Clear roles and responsibilities for all AI assets and operational decisions.  
3. **Ethical Oversight:** Policies enforce fairness, non-bias, and compliance with recognized AI ethics standards.  
4. **Risk Management:** Continuous identification and mitigation of operational, behavioral, and systemic risks.  
5. **Interoperability:** Frameworks, datasets, and policies must align with NIST and ISO standards.  
6. **Continuous Monitoring:** Establish KPIs, drift detection, and corrective action mechanisms.

## 4. Governance Implementation Guidelines

1. **Define Roles & Responsibilities:** Map all AI assets to governance roles.  
2. **Establish Policies:** Include decision-making authority, ethical guidelines, and compliance requirements.  
3. **Monitoring & Reporting:** Track KPIs, drift, and compliance through dashboards and logs.  
4. **Corrective Mechanisms:** Document mitigation actions, retraining, and process adjustments.  
5. **Periodic Review:** Quarterly evaluation against NIST/ISO frameworks.  
6. **Documentation & Crosswalks:** Maintain version-controlled artifacts that align HHI terminology with NIST/ISO language.  
7. **Stakeholder Education:** Deliver training modules and track comprehension scores.  
8. **Regulatory Engagement:** Submit artifacts, reports, and crosswalks to relevant AI governance associations.
