Governance Before Agents

Most AI systems are built around models, automation, and agents first. Governance is often introduced later as policy or compliance documentation.

The Hollow House Institute approaches the problem differently. Governance must exist as infrastructure before autonomous systems are allowed to operate.

Human authority remains the final decision boundary in all AI-mediated environments.

What We Do

HHI conducts independent AI governance and compliance audits for organizations operating complex AI systems and platforms.

Our work focuses on measuring how risk, instability, and behavioral drift accumulate over time in AI-assisted systems.

This approach evaluates longitudinal behavioral patterns rather than relying solely on static datasets, sentiment analysis, or snapshot audits.

Why It Matters

Most AI failures do not occur when controls are bypassed.

They occur when controls are passed repeatedly while behavior quietly shifts.

Over time, these shifts accumulate until systems behave in ways their operators no longer fully understand.

Governance infrastructure makes those shifts visible.
